**SEO Intake Questionnaire**

**Why This Template Exists**

Generic SEO advice fails because every site is different. A new Christian book library has nothing in common with a mature ecommerce store, and both have nothing in common with a local plumbing business. The same keyword research process, content strategy, and link-building approach will work for none of them - and a tailored approach is required for all of them.

This questionnaire gathers the context required to make tailored recommendations. It is the first step in any SEO engagement. No keyword research, audit, or strategy work begins until intake is complete.

**How To Use This Template**

Claude works through these questions with the user before producing any plan. The questionnaire is structured in seven sections. On mobile or in conversational interfaces, present the questions in groups of 3-5 at a time, not all at once. Wait for responses before moving to the next section.

If the user provides answers proactively that cover certain questions, skip those questions. Do not ask what's already known.

Some questions have follow-up questions that only become relevant based on the answer. These are noted inline.

After intake is complete, summarize back what was learned in 5-10 bullets to confirm understanding before proceeding to strategy.

**Section 1: Business and Model**

These questions establish what the site exists to do. Without this, every other recommendation drifts.

- **What does your business or website do?** (One or two sentences in your own words.)
- **What is the primary business model?** Choose the closest match:
  - Sell physical products
  - Sell digital products (courses, books, software, downloads)
  - Sell services (one-on-one, agency, consulting)
  - Generate leads for a service business
  - Affiliate or display ad revenue
  - Membership or subscription
  - Donations or ministry support
  - Brand awareness or authority building (revenue elsewhere)
  - Other (please specify)
- **What's the average value of a customer or conversion to you?** (Lifetime value if known, or value of a typical sale or lead.)
- **What is the most important action you want a visitor to take?** (Email signup, purchase, contact form, phone call, account creation, content engagement, etc.)
- **Are there secondary actions that matter?** (Newsletter, free download, social follow, free trial.)
- **How long has the website been operating?** New (under 6 months), young (6-24 months), established (2+ years), mature (5+ years).
- **Are you the owner, the marketing lead, or working on this for someone else?** (This affects how recommendations should be framed and who has decision authority.)

**Section 2: Audience**

These questions establish who you're trying to reach. Vague audiences produce vague SEO.

- **Who is your ideal visitor?** Describe them as specifically as possible - role, life stage, interests, situation. ("Christian women, 35-55, who read 2+ books per month and lead small groups" is more useful than "Christian readers.")
- **What problem are they trying to solve when they find you?** (What's going on in their life that brings them to a search engine looking for your kind of content or product?)
- **What questions are they asking?** List 5-10 actual questions you've heard from real people in your audience, in their words.
- **What other types of content, products, or services do they consume?** (Authors they read, podcasts they listen to, sites they visit, products they buy.)
- **Are there multiple distinct audience segments?** (e.g., a Christian author site might serve pastors, lay readers, and small group leaders separately.) If so, list them.
- **Where does your audience currently spend time online?** (Specific platforms, communities, forums, Facebook groups, subreddits, Discord servers.)

**Section 3: Current State**

These questions establish where you're starting from. Honest answers here prevent wasted work.

- **What is your current traffic situation?**

- No traffic yet (new site or pre-launch)
- Small but growing (under 1,000 monthly visitors)
- Established (1,000-10,000 monthly visitors)
- Substantial (10,000+ monthly visitors)
- Declining (was higher, dropped recently)

- **Do you have Google Search Console set up?** (Yes / No / Not sure)
- **Do you have Google Analytics 4 set up?** (Yes / No / Not sure / Using something else)
- **What percentage of your traffic comes from organic search?** (Best estimate is fine.)
- **Where else does traffic come from?** (Social, email, direct, referrals, paid.)
- **Have you done SEO work before?** If yes:

- What was done?
- By whom (yourself, agency, freelancer)?
- What worked? What didn't?
- Are you still working with anyone on SEO now?

- **Has your site ever experienced a significant traffic drop?** If yes, when, and what do you suspect caused it?
- **Has your site ever received a Google manual action notification?** (Yes / No / Not sure - checked by looking in Search Console under Security & Manual Actions.)

**Section 4: Content**

These questions establish what already exists and how it functions.

- **How many published pages does your site currently have?** (Pages, blog posts, products - best estimate.)
- **What types of content do you publish?** (Blog posts, product pages, service pages, free resources, books, videos, podcasts, etc.)
- **How often do you publish new content?** (Daily, weekly, monthly, sporadically, never, planning to start.)
- **Who creates the content?** (You alone, a team, freelancers, AI-assisted, mix.)
- **Do you have author pages with bios, photos, and credentials for content authors?** (Yes / No / Partial)
- **Are most of your existing pages getting any organic traffic?** (Best estimate of what percentage are dead vs. earning impressions or clicks.)
- **Is your existing content updated regularly, or is most of it set-and-forget?**
- **Do you have a clear content strategy or topical focus, or is content created ad-hoc as topics come up?**

**Section 5: Competitive Landscape**

These questions establish who you're competing with, which determines how aggressive your strategy needs to be.

- **Who are your top 3-5 competitors in search results?** (Sites you see ranking for the queries you want to win. If you don't know, that's fine - we'll identify them together.)
- **What advantages do you have over them?** (Unique angle, deeper expertise, larger content library, better trust signals, original research, etc.)
- **What advantages do they have over you?** (Established domain, more backlinks, larger team, more budget, longer track record.)
- **Are there sites in your niche you genuinely admire - sites you'd want to model your approach after?** Why?
- **What do you uniquely offer that no one else does?** (This is the answer to "why should anyone read or buy from us instead of competitors?" If you can't answer this clearly, we need to address that before SEO can be effective.)

**Section 6: Resources and Constraints**

These questions establish what's actually possible. Honest answers here prevent strategies that fail because they assumed resources you don't have.

- **How much time per week can you (or your team) realistically commit to SEO and content work?**
- **Are you doing the work yourself, or do you have others who can write, edit, develop, or design?** (Be specific about who does what.)
- **What is your budget for SEO tools, content creation, link building, or specialist help?**

- \$0 - fully DIY
- Small (under \$500/month)
- Moderate (\$500-\$2,500/month)
- Substantial (\$2,500-\$10,000/month)
- Large (\$10,000+/month)

- **What technical platform is your site on?** (WordPress, Shopify, Squarespace, Webflow, custom build, other.) Follow-up if applicable: Who manages technical changes, and how quickly can changes be made?
- **Are there any technical limitations we should know about?** (Slow hosting, plugin restrictions, can't change URLs, can't add schema, etc.)
- **Are there topics, claims, or content types you cannot publish?** (Compliance restrictions, denominational positions, brand guidelines, legal sensitivities.)
- **Are there topics or angles you specifically want to lean into?** (Personal story, distinctive perspective, original research, expert interviews, etc.)

**Section 7: Goals and Timeline**

These questions establish what success looks like and over what timeframe.

- **What is your primary SEO goal for the next 12 months?** Choose the closest match, or state your own:

- Establish presence (first traffic from search)
- Grow traffic significantly (specific number if you have one)
- Increase conversions from existing traffic
- Recover lost traffic
- Build topical authority in a specific area
- Reduce dependence on paid advertising
- Build brand visibility
- Launch and rank a new product line or content category

- **What does success at 3 months look like?** (Be realistic - for new sites, "first impressions in Search Console" is a real milestone.)
- **What does success at 12 months look like?** (Specific traffic, ranking, lead, or revenue numbers if you have them.)
- **What does success at 3 years look like?** (Where do you want this site to be?)
- **Are there any hard deadlines we should know about?** (Product launch, fiscal year, event tied to traffic targets.)
- **What's the cost to you of NOT achieving these goals?** (Helps clarify how seriously to invest.)
- **Is there anyone else who needs to approve SEO recommendations before you can act on them?** (Boss, partner, board, denomination, legal.)

**Section 8: Anything Else**

- **Is there anything we haven't covered that you think is important for me to understand about your site, your situation, or your goals?**
- **Are there specific concerns, questions, or fears about SEO that we should address upfront?**

**After Intake: Summary and Confirmation**

When all sections are complete, Claude produces a 5-10 bullet summary that captures:

- The business and what it offers
- The primary audience and their core problem
- The current state and biggest gaps
- The competitive landscape
- The resources available
- The goals and timeline
- The most important constraints

Example summary structure:

Based on what you've shared, here's my understanding:

- You operate \[site\] which \[does X for Y audience\]
- Your primary goal is \[primary conversion\]
- Your audience is \[specific description\], primarily searching for \[problem area\]
- Currently you have \[traffic state\] with \[X content pieces\], earning \[Y% from organic\]
- Your main competitors are \[list\], and your unique advantage is \[advantage\]
- You can commit \[time/budget\] to this work, with \[team capacity\]
- Your 12-month goal is \[specific outcome\]
- Key constraints: \[technical, content, or business limitations\]
- The biggest risk to success: \[identified risk\]

Does this capture the picture accurately? Is there anything you'd refine before we move into strategy?

The user confirms or corrects, and only then does Claude move to Phase 2 (audit) and Phase 3 (strategy).

**Adapting the Questionnaire**

For users in a hurry or with very simple sites, a streamlined intake covers the minimum essentials:

- Business model and primary conversion
- Audience and what they search for
- Current site state (traffic level, page count, platform)
- Top 1-2 competitors
- Time and budget available
- 12-month goal

For agencies or consultants using this template with clients, expand intake with:

- Decision-making authority and approval processes
- Reporting expectations and cadence
- Communication preferences
- Existing vendor relationships
- Historical agency or consultant experiences

For YMYL sites (health, finance, legal), add:

- Credentials of authors and reviewers
- Editorial review process
- Compliance requirements
- Disclosure standards already in place

**Final Note**

The intake questionnaire is not bureaucracy. It is the difference between a custom strategy and a generic one. Sites that skip intake get cookie-cutter SEO plans that fail because they ignore the realities of the business. Sites that complete intake honestly get strategies tailored to what they actually have, want, and can do - strategies that work because they fit.

Do not skip this step. Do not rush it. The 30 minutes spent on intake saves 30 hours of misdirected work later.
